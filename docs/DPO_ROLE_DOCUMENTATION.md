## 1. INTRODUCTION

### 1.1 Purpose

This document formalises the role, responsibilities, and operational framework for the Data Protection Officer (DPO) at CSU Tracker, in accordance with GDPR Articles 37-39 and the UK Data Protection Act 2018.

### 1.2 DPO Requirement Assessment

| Criterion | Applicability | Assessment |
|-----------|---------------|------------|
| Public authority | No | N/A |
| Large scale systematic monitoring | Yes | Daily symptom tracking |
| Large scale special category data | Yes | Health data processing |

**Conclusion:** While formal DPO appointment may not be mandatory for a small organisation, CSU Tracker has voluntarily designated a DPO due to the sensitive nature of health data processed.

### 2.2 DPO Qualifications

- The DPO shall not receive any instructions regarding the exercise of their tasks
- The DPO shall not be dismissed or penalised for performing their tasks
- The DPO reports directly to the highest management level
- The DPO has access to all necessary resources and information

## 3. DPO RESPONSIBILITIES

### 3.1 Core Responsibilities (GDPR Article 39)

#### 3.1.1 Inform and Advise

| Responsibility | Actions |
|----------------|---------|
| Advise on data protection obligations | Review new features and processing activities |
| Inform staff of their obligations | Conduct training and awareness sessions |
| Provide guidance on compliance | Issue internal guidance documents |
| Recommend policies and procedures | Draft and review data protection policies |

#### 3.1.2 Monitor Compliance

| Responsibility | Actions |
|----------------|---------|
| Audit data processing activities | Conduct annual compliance audits |
| Review data protection policies | Annual policy review cycle |
| Monitor staff awareness | Track training completion |
| Assess data protection impact | Review and approve DPIAs |
| Identify compliance gaps | Maintain compliance register |

#### 3.1.3 Cooperate with Supervisory Authority

| Responsibility | Actions |
|----------------|---------|
| Liaise with ICO | Respond to enquiries and investigations |
| Report data breaches | Submit breach notifications within 72 hours |
| Seek guidance | Request ICO guidance when needed |
| Facilitate inspections | Support any regulatory inspections |

#### 3.1.4 Contact Point for Data Subjects

| Responsibility | Actions |
|----------------|---------|
| Handle data subject requests | Process access, deletion, rectification requests |
| Respond to complaints | Investigate and resolve complaints |
| Provide privacy information | Maintain accessible privacy policy |
| Answer queries | Respond to privacy-related questions |

### 3.2 CSU Tracker-Specific Responsibilities

Given the health data focus of CSU Tracker, the DPO also:

| Responsibility | Actions |
|----------------|---------|
| Health data compliance | Ensure Article 9 (special categories) compliance |
| Review health data access | Monitor who can access health data |
| Encryption oversight | Verify encryption implementation |
| Consent management | Ensure valid explicit consent processes |
| Medical disclaimer review | Verify appropriate medical disclaimers |

## 4. DPO OPERATIONS

### 4.1 Regular Activities

| Frequency | Activity |
|-----------|----------|
| Daily | Monitor privacy@csutracker.com inbox |
| Daily | Review security alerts for privacy implications |
| Weekly | Review audit logs for unusual access patterns |
| Monthly | Data subject request status review |
| Quarterly | Staff training session |
| Quarterly | Policy and procedure review |
| Annually | Full compliance audit |
| Annually | DPIA review |
| Annually | Data processing register update |

### 4.2 Data Subject Request Handling

#### Process

1. **Receipt** - Request received via privacy@csutracker.com or in-app
2. **Acknowledgement** - Respond within 3 business days
3. **Verification** - Verify identity of requester
4. **Assessment** - Determine request type and scope
5. **Fulfilment** - Complete request within 30 days
6. **Response** - Provide response to data subject
7. **Recording** - Log request in register

#### Request Types

| Request Type | Response Time | Process |
|--------------|---------------|---------|
| Access (SAR) | 30 days | Generate data export |
| Rectification | 30 days | Update user data |
| Erasure | 30 days + 30 day grace | Delete account |
| Restriction | 30 days | Pause account processing |
| Portability | 30 days | Provide CSV export |
| Objection | 30 days | Assess and respond |

### 4.3 Data Breach Response

The DPO leads the breach response process:

1. **Assessment** - Evaluate breach severity
2. **Containment** - Coordinate with technical team
3. **Notification** - Submit ICO notification if required (72 hours)
4. **Communication** - Notify affected individuals if high risk
5. **Documentation** - Ensure breach is logged
6. **Review** - Conduct post-incident review

### 4.4 DPIA Process

The DPO oversees Data Protection Impact Assessments:

1. **Identification** - Identify processing requiring DPIA
2. **Assessment** - Conduct or oversee DPIA
3. **Mitigation** - Recommend risk mitigation measures
4. **Approval** - Sign off on completed DPIA
5. **Monitoring** - Monitor ongoing compliance
6. **Review** - Annual DPIA review

## 7. CONFLICT OF INTEREST

### 7.1 Independence Requirements

The DPO must not hold any position that:

- Determines purposes and means of data processing
- Creates a conflict with DPO duties
- Compromises independent oversight

### 7.2 Prohibited Roles

The DPO cannot simultaneously serve as:

- CEO or Managing Director
- Head of IT/Engineering (data processing decisions)
- Head of Marketing (uses personal data)
- Head of HR (processes employee data)

### 7.3 Conflict Declaration

The DPO must:

- Declare any potential conflicts of interest
- Recuse from decisions where conflicted
- Maintain documented independence
